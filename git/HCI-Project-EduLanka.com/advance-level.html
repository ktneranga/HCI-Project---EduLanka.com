A/L Exam

The Sri Lankan Advanced Level (A-level), is a General Certificate of Education (GCE) qualification exam in Sri Lanka, similar to the British Advanced Level, conducted annually by the Department of Examinations of the of Education. It is usually taken by students during the optional final two years of collegiate level (grade 12 and Ministry 13 or external (non-school) candidates), after they have completed GCE Ordinary Level exams. The majority of candidates enter the exams via their respective schools, while candidates who have finished school education can also apply as private applicants. The qualification also serves as an entrance requirement for Sri Lankan state universities. The exams are held in three mediums: Sinhala, Tamil and English. G.C.E. Advanced Level (A/L) major examination in Sri Lanka. Government University selection is done based on A/L exam results.
